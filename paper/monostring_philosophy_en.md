## Introduction: From the Multitude to the One
Many scientists might consider the question of a hypothetical primary element to be purely philosophical, arguing that a philosophical idea holds no value unless mathematically aligned with Quantum Field Theory and String Theory. However, is it justifiable to restrict ourselves exclusively to existing mathematical frameworks when dealing with an object that is the deep root cause of all other processes? I believe that for the most elementary objects, the logic of simplicity and universality is far more appropriate.

The very concept of an "initial multitude" at the beginning of time is unnatural. If a multitude of elements (e.g., electrons) existed initially, and they were not connected to a Single Origin, why did they turn out to be absolutely identical clones? The fact of an initial multitude logically implies a cause for that multitude. Reasoning logically, one can arrive at a profound conclusion: in the Beginning, it only makes sense for a single element to exist.

**The essence of the hypothesis is that everything that exists is woven by the oscillations of a single, solitary Basic Element (the Protoelement, or "Monostring").** It sequentially resides in various discrete oscillatory states. A conceptual prototype for this is the one-electron universe theory of J. Wheeler and R. Feynman, as well as the holistic concept of David Bohm. In our hypothesis, we push this idea to its absolute limit: all space, time, and matter are manifestations of a single pulsating object.

## 1. Ontology: Energy, Cycles, and Entropy
The mere fact that a primary object exists implies it possesses a non-zero measure of being (energy). An absolutely motionless element simply cannot exist — instead of it, there would be "NOTHING." The energy of the Protoelement is realized as continuous oscillation.

*   **Discreteness and the Pendulum Analogy:** The state of the Protoelement is a continuous shift from non-equilibrium phases to equilibrium ones. This is analogous to the pendulum effect in a clock mechanism: the continuous energy of the spring generates discrete cycles. This phase shift creates the minimal period (quantum) of the Protoelement's state.
*   **Chaos and the Ocean Analogy:** A certain chaotic component, inherent to any object with non-zero energy, "rocks" the Protoelement. Just as gusty winds generate combinations of waves of varying lengths on the surface of the ocean, this instability generates resonances, overtones, and larger cycles of oscillation.
*   **The Global Cycle and Time:** The largest cycle returns the system to a conditionally initial state. This Main Cycle forms the quantum of macroscopic time. If the cycle were perfect, time (as a process of change) would not exist — there would only be a monotonous, looped existence. It is the accumulated measure of instability that breaks the loop and pushes the arrow of time forward.
*   **The Entropy Limit:** Because the Protoelement is an absolutely closed system with finite (constant) energy, the cycle of state changes is equivalent to this energy. The system evolves from minimal entropy at the "super-zero of being" to its average value, but **entropy will never reach a maximum** (heat death is impossible), as the energy of the closed Protoelement never depletes.

## 2. Emergent Space and the Illusion of Dimensions
In the Monostring hypothesis, the N-dimensional space we are accustomed to is a fiction.
*   **The CRT Illusion:** The concept of time does not exist for the Protoelement itself. Relative to all our processes, it "oscillates" at an incredible frequency, forming all particles, much like the electron beam in an old CRT television that sequentially scans all pixels on the screen many times a second, creating the illusion of a solid, simultaneous picture.
*   **The Fractal Matrix:** To grasp the scale of these states (a googol, $10^{100}$, or more), one can use a fractal analogy: imagine a cube made of 1,000 dots. Then shrink it to the size of a single dot and build a new cube out of those dots (now a million dots). This process of unfolding a single dimension into a multidimensional structure creates the fabric of space.
*   **Linear Dimensions as Energy:** Linear lengths are conventional. The spatial separation between objects is caused by the energy (or time) the Protoelement must expend to transition from one state to another. We perceive this difference in states as three-dimensional distance.
*   **Paradoxes of Relativity:** A moving object is like a wave of changes stretched in the direction of motion. The integral mass of the object stretched along this wave increases. The speed of light limit arises because the Protoelement physically does not have enough "computational" capacity to render state changes across space faster than the speed at which it completes its global cycle.

## 3. Quantum Mechanics: Waves, Matter, and Entanglement
*   **Matter and Interactions:** Fermions are standing waves of resonances — the unchanging part of the harmonics from cycle to cycle. Bosons are traveling waves of changes, running along the "string" and driven by the energy of the Protoelement.
*   **Matter and Antimatter:** These can be viewed as oscillations of the Protoelement's states with strictly opposite phases. When they collide, annihilation occurs — a massive release of transition energy.
*   **Probability (The Sieve Analogy):** At the fundamental level, there is no true "randomness." But as discrepancies accumulate, the process resembles sifting grains through a sieve: in each cycle, only a fraction of the grains pass through the mesh in pseudo-random places. This creates the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics.
*   **Entanglement (Lock and Key):** Entangled particles are antipodes (superpositions). If an action is performed on one particle, the signal (traveling wave) does not run across the entire physical space. Based on phase matching (the "lock and key" principle), the signal jumps directly to the entangled particle. Crucially, **this happens within a single global cycle of the Protoelement**, which is why, for us (macroscopic observers), the wavefunction collapse appears as an instantaneous, non-local miracle.

## 4. Cosmogenesis: The Big Bang and Black Holes
How did our world emerge from a single oscillation?
*   **The Big Bang:** In the minus-infinity of time (the "super-zero"), there was a single, perfect harmonic oscillation. Gradually, it dissolved into a symphony of harmonics. Our space was likely a "formatted" field filled with paired antipode-states. The accumulation of resonances led to an avalanche-like annihilation of these pairs. The colossal energy released tore the metric apart (the Big Bang), leaving a surplus of states without antipodes (matter) and triggering cosmic inflation driven by the complexification of network connections.
*   **Gravity and Black Holes:** The states of the Protoelement naturally tend to align into a single "super-harmonic" with minimal degrees of freedom — this is gravity. A black hole is a mega-ensemble where the Protoelement's states have absolutely zero degrees of freedom. Inside it, there are no changes, and therefore, no flow of time. An interesting corollary: since the system is closed, the concentration (compression) of degrees of freedom inside black holes automatically forces the rest of the network to expand, which may explain dark energy and the accelerating expansion of the Universe.
